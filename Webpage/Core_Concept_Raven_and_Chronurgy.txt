Core Concept — Raven and Chronurgy

Raven's early magic is rooted in control, awareness, anchoring, and survival. Those instincts shape the way he first understands magic and explain why his earliest Arcane Techniques feel grounded, defensive, and inwardly centered.

But Raven's true magical passion is not safety.

Eldrin points Raven toward Abjuration because it is disciplined, practical, and protective. It is the kind of magic a cautious teacher would want a gifted child to learn.

Raven, however, is drawn toward Chronurgy.

This is not because time magic is flashy or powerful. It is because the death of his father left Raven with a wound that shaped the way he thinks about magic itself. If Raven could hold a moment still, bottle a second of time, or reach back into the instant something was lost, he would. That longing makes time his deepest magical question.

Raven studies Chronurgy because it represents the impossible hope at the center of his life: the idea that if a moment could be understood deeply enough, it might be changed.

Over time, Raven will learn that time cannot simply be reset to undo his father's death. But he still goes far enough down that path for Chronurgy to become his school.

So the distinction is:

Abjuration is the school Raven is guided toward.

Chronurgy is the school Raven chooses.

His foundation remains control. His pursuit becomes time.
