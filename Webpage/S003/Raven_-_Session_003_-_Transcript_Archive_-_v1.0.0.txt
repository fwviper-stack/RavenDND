Raven - Session 003 - Transcript Archive - v1.0.0
Session transcript archive for Session 003 under Raven Protocol v6.3.1

Archive Note
This transcript archive preserves the major dialogue and scene flow of Session 003 in condensed transcript form. It is not a word-for-word raw export of every sentence, but it is built to preserve the session's actual scene order, major statements, and continuity-significant language.

Session Scope
Start: Raven leaves the general shop with plain incense after Session 002.
End: Long rest counted after healer intervention, with Raven asleep and stable enough for session closure.

[Scene 1 - Leaving town and entering the woods]
Raven leaves the general shop with the plain incense and heads toward the forest under an ordinary scavenging pattern.
Raven admits inwardly that he also wants to see signs of elves.
The familiar stays close as the safer edge of the wood gives way to deeper, less familiar ground.
The forest first rewards him with resin scent, bark scores, and a promising quiet.

[Scene 2 - The grove reveals itself]
Raven notices that the stillness is wrong.
The ground looks too open in the wrong places.
He catches pale movement and presses farther.
The grove springs its first snare.
Raven partially evades, realizes this is not a beast but terrain acting like a predator, and tries to escape the woodland mouth rather than fight one body.
He is badly hurt but does not die in the first exchange.

[Scene 3 - Escape, hiding, and the call for help]
Raven uses Unseen Posture and Flow State to slip out of the primary kill zone.
Once partially clear, he sends his familiar to fetch Lena.
He hides in rough cover, stabilizes with Second Wind, uses Soft Awareness and Anchor Self, and tries to slow his heart against the poison.
He suspects the grove's sap is still in him and begins resisting it internally.

[Scene 4 - Lena arrives and chooses the ring]
Lena reaches Raven in time, furious and terrified.
She confirms he is too badly hurt to trust ordinary extraction.
Raven senses they are not alone in the woods.
Lena decides ordinary treatment is not enough.
She tells Raven, "Live long enough to be angry."
She places the Ring of Unfinished Grace on his hand.
Immediate collapse is halted.
Raven survives the move out of the woods.

[Scene 5 - The walk home]
Raven and Lena talk through pain, fear, and embarrassment on the way home.
Raven realizes not carrying Joren's sword may have saved his life because having a blade might have tempted him to stand and fight the grove.
Lena confirms that not-bad and safe are not the same thing.
Raven asks questions about boys, elves, magic, and why he feels the way he does.
Lena answers plainly that boys can like boys and that Raven can bring those questions to her again when he is less poisoned.
Raven reports that the ring does not feel like a cure. It feels like it is telling him to continue.
Lena immediately understands that improvement is not the same thing as safety.

[Scene 6 - Home treatment begins]
At home, Lena removes blood-and-sap-soiled clothes carefully and cleans the wounds.
Raven identifies that the poison remains active.
The ring has reduced bleeding and collapse but has not solved the real problem.
Raven asks for Eldrin.
The familiar is sent to fetch him.
Raven asks what the ring is and later asks if Lena will someday tell him how she came by it.
Lena promises she will tell him when he is stable enough.

[Scene 7 - Eldrin arrives]
Eldrin arrives urgently and assesses the situation.
He identifies the ring as divine rather than arcane.
Raven tells him the ring is "holding the line" and that it first saved life but now is preventing the body from advancing normally.
Eldrin recognizes the ring as broad divine stabilization rather than true cure.
They test a tonic that contests the poison.
Raven reports that the potion pushes the poison, the poison pushes back, and the ring beneath it all seems to insist that he continue.
Raven becomes frustrated with Eldrin's wording and demands plain speech.
Eldrin eventually admits Raven's reporting has been better than his questions.

[Scene 8 - Theoretical arguments in the sickroom]
Raven argues about wizard healing, necromancy, divine spell structure, and whether healing-like arcane spells could be engineered.
He identifies necromancy as the school touching most closely on body, soul, and animating force from the darker side.
He later jokes that if lightning struck him for blasphemy, perhaps the ring would simply force him to continue walking with the poison cooked in place.
Lena and Eldrin repeatedly redirect him back to survival and plain speech.

[Scene 9 - OOC pause and XP correction]
Out of character, Raven identifies that delayed XP posting risks losing earned progress if a session ends unexpectedly.
Protocol use is corrected: future XP should be awarded in micro-increments during play rather than delayed to later beats.
Session 003 XP is caught up mid-session before play resumes.

[Scene 10 - Recovery talk while waiting for the healer]
Raven talks with Lena about books, tears, druid complaints, offensive magic restraint, sword-and-magic integration, and Eldrin's need to learn how to run if he insists on standing near rifts.
Raven says he does not yet trust himself with large offensive magic, especially Fireball, because he would not trust his judgment with a spell that could burn down a murder-grove.
Lena strongly approves this restraint.
Raven also says he wants to invent a spell to cure poison and illness because both are horrible and have to be removed.
Lena protects the dream while insisting he live long enough to do it.

[Scene 11 - Ring history and family truth]
At a stable moment, Raven asks Lena how she came by the ring.
Lena reveals that years after Raven's father died, a false-merciful holy man used the ring on another child.
Adults around that crisis failed badly enough that she ended up keeping the ring hidden rather than letting worse hands hold it.
She confirms that the ring did eventually come off that child, but not by ordinary means.
Raven tells Lena that magic can make him feel as if his father is still near, just as holding a sword can make him feel her strength.
Lena answers that love and training leave marks without reducing a child to inheritance alone.

[Scene 12 - The vow]
Raven tells Lena that the day would not have gone differently even if Identify had been available because she already knew enough to choose the ring.
He tells her he loves her for choosing him, even if it means he has to be a child longer.
Lena answers that she is sorry for the price, not for paying it, and vows that the curse will not simply be accepted forever.
This is one of the session's central emotional anchors.

[Scene 13 - Waiting sleep]
Raven finally drifts into real sleep while Lena keeps watch.
Eldrin goes for a healer because arcane management alone is no longer enough.

[Scene 14 - Healer arrives]
Eldrin returns with a carefully chosen cleric healer.
She immediately distinguishes between a ring that first preserved life and a ring that now preserves continuation.
Raven gives key reports:
- the ring first kept him alive
- now it is keeping his body from advancing normally
- the poison feels cleaner under the healer's magic
- the ring is watching but not resisting openly
The healer concludes that the ring is not sentient like a person but attentive like a trap or a judgment observing conditions.
She works around the ring's logic rather than against it directly.

[Scene 15 - The poison turns]
The healer's cleansing gradually makes the poison feel cleaner.
The ring stays watchful but does not tighten into open resistance.
Raven eventually stops reporting because the ring's behavior is no longer changing.
The poison loses comfort, then starts to give way.
Sleep begins to feel safe rather than dangerous.

[Scene 16 - Session closure state]
The group allows Raven to sleep because the poison is finally fading rather than merely being held in place.
Long rest is later counted at session end.
Raven levels to Fighter 2 / Wizard 3.
The ring remains on his hand.
The poison is no longer winning the field.
The curse remains unresolved but is now an openly shared problem rather than a hidden one.
