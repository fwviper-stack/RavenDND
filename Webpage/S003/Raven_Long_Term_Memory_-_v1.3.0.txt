Foundational Loss, Control Hunger, and Refusal to Yield
Session 003 deepens the father-loss continuity in a major way. Raven no longer only fears being shaped by magic instead of shaping it rightly. Under poison, ring pressure, and real threat, he explicitly recognizes that some of his instinctive arcane workings may feel inherited from his father in the same way his sword-feel comes from Lena. This does not become prophecy. It becomes a more mature long-term motive: inheritance can be felt without being obeyed.

Maternal Love as Chosen Cost
Session 003 adds one of the clearest enduring truths in the campaign so far: Lena will choose Raven over principle, caution, and future clean consequences if that is what survival demands. She places the ring on him, later tells him plainly that she is sorry for the price but not for paying it, and Raven answers with full understanding rather than blame. This should be remembered as a defining bond moment, not merely a crisis reaction.

The Curse as Shared Fight Rather Than Private Shame
Session 003 changes the long-term shape of the ring from hidden dangerous object to shared family war. Raven and Lena explicitly vow that the child-holding judgment will not simply be accepted as forever. This matters because the curse is no longer only something done to Raven. It is now something Raven, Lena, and their wider support structure intend to study, oppose, and eventually undo.

Power, Restraint, and Judgment
A major enduring identity note from Session 003 is Raven's explicit refusal to rush toward destructive power he does not yet trust himself to wield wisely. He says plainly that he is not ready for magic on the scale of Fireball because he would not trust his judgment with a spell capable of burning down a murder-grove. This should be remembered as one of the clearest proofs that Raven's hunger for power is checked by a real ethic of self-distrust and consequence, not only by fear or lack of access.

Integration Instead of Purity
Session 003 also clarifies Raven's long-term combat ethic. In training, he wants the sword lesson honest and therefore kept clean of magic unless the lesson itself changes. In survival, he no longer believes honesty means purity of tools. This emerging doctrine - honest separation in practice, integrated use in real danger - should be treated as part of his enduring growth rather than a one-night improvisation.

Research Motive Expansion
Raven's long-term research identity expands in Session 003. He openly states a desire to someday invent a spell to cure poison and illness because both are horrible and have to be removed. He also identifies Identify as a sensible next-priority spell after a night ruled by hidden properties and dangerous unknowns. These are not idle lines. They mark a durable turn toward practical anti-harm magic as part of Raven's future arc.

Enduring Motive Structure
Session 003 strengthens several long-term motives already present:
- the need to earn rightness instead of trusting counterfeit ease
- the refusal to let power define truth on its own
- the willingness to call for help before pride becomes death
- the desire to understand dangerous magic in order to reduce harm rather than merely admire it
- the need to preserve love and identity inside inherited power rather than surrender either one

Long Term Retrieval Notes
Use this file when a future scene turns on why Raven links healing, restraint, inheritance, and anti-corruption work so strongly; why Lena's choice with the ring matters as more than one crisis decision; or why Raven's future magical growth should not be written as simple hunger for larger force.
