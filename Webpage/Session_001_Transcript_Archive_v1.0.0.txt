Opening State
Raven begins Session 1 under protocol governance with Edrin as his active magical mentor, his mother as the defining practical authority in his life, and the forest already carrying active tear and thin-place danger. He wants Find Familiar as his first real wizard spell, but enters the session under teaching that still emphasizes caution, boundaries, and shutdown more than formal spell structure.

Scene 1 — Edrin’s map and the first refusal
Raven immediately challenges Edrin’s hand-drawn tear map, asking what the circles mean and what the distance between them actually is. Edrin answers that it is a warning map, not a traveler’s map, and that thin places must be measured by anchors, landmarks, silence, and inconsistency rather than by merchant-distance alone. This establishes the session’s core dynamic: Raven keeps demanding usable thought rather than accepting atmosphere as instruction.

Scene 2 — The split-boulder tear
Raven and Edrin go to the nearest marked tear. Raven uses Soft Awareness with notable restraint. He identifies the tear as a wound-like seam that draws sound, calm, and attention inward, suppresses the ordinary behavior of the forest, and feels anchored yet not fully still. He concludes it is healing inward or drawing closed rather than widening. Edrin treats the reading as serious and begins trusting Raven’s field perception as something more than lucky instinct.

Scene 3 — Weave, teaching, and why his magic feels wrong in books
Raven confronts Edrin with the fact that the library’s idea of spells and the Weave do not feel like what Edrin has been teaching. Edrin answers that Raven’s magic may not enter the world through the same tidy structure that formal wizard traditions assume. He suggests that Raven’s responses may be more instinctive, more survival-shaped, and more inwardly aligned than formal city spellcraft. Raven pushes back on any explanation that sounds like another excuse not to teach actual spells.

Scene 4 — The walk back and the first hard suspicion
As the lesson ends, Raven increasingly suspects that Edrin’s teaching is being slowed not only by care for Raven but by Edrin’s own distrust of his magic. This tension remains active through the whole session and becomes central later.

Scene 5 — Homecoming and the mother as interpreter of danger
Back home, Raven tells his mother what Edrin taught him. She confirms that learning how to end, dismiss, contain, or survive a spell before learning to start it is the difference between work and disaster. She also says openly that she distrusts both magic and men who teach it to boys unless they prove themselves worth trusting. Raven asks whether she distrusts Edrin because of magic or because of him. She answers: both, because teachers shape and magic kills.

Scene 6 — The father’s death becomes specific
Raven reveals he remembers his father’s death as magic that burned him from within. He remembers the wound, and he remembers his father’s final words: “not just blade.” His mother confirms that she and Raven’s father were adventurers together. She says his father was good at the arcane until he was not, making Raven increasingly suspect magical backlash or overreach. She implies strongly that his father’s talent was more like sorcery than wizardry. This reframes the father-loss anchor event in a way that will matter to all later arcane study.

Scene 7 — Work, stable labor, and the material cost of magic
Raven begins thinking practically about work in Neverwinter. He tries the library and earns occasional small tasks there, but finds steadier work at the stables. Stable work grounds him. He also begins thinking seriously about the cost of a spellbook and the possibility of building one by hand if he cannot buy one. Scrap paper becomes valuable to him as a way to empty his thoughts before sleep.

Scene 8 — The mother’s hidden trust and the four-hour boundary
Raven and his mother discuss her forest boundary in practical terms: she tracks how far she can find him within about four hours before dark. Raven realizes she may have tracked him farther than he knew when he was with Edrin. She admits some of it. This reinforces that her caution is logistical love rather than abstract control.

Scene 9 — Edrin confronted directly about fear and fault
In later lessons Raven pushes Edrin harder than before. He says aloud that Edrin acts like a man afraid to cast, that he teaches endings and shutdown because he no longer trusts his own skill, and that at least one tear in the forest may be Edrin’s fault. Edrin finally admits the split-boulder tear is tied to his own failed boundary-contact working. He says he forced a seam in the world while trying to test or study it and left the forest with one more wound than it had before. He admits that much of his caution belongs to his own scar before it belongs to Raven.

Scene 10 — Mother, father, and old roads
Raven’s mother confirms more of her shared past with Raven’s father: they traveled dangerous roads, guarded caravans, took contracts, and lived the kind of life that gets called adventuring by people who like the word more than the work. She says she stayed when Raven came; his father kept going longer than he should have. She confirms big libraries exist beyond Neverwinter and that Waterdeep in particular would have many more shelves, more mages, and more ways to learn than one old wizard.

Scene 11 — Sorcery as a live possibility
Raven reasons aloud that wizardly training and sorcerous inheritance are not the same thing. If his father’s talent was sorcery, then learning wizard spells will not automatically teach control of inherited power. His mother agrees. She asks Edrin to test him for possible sorcerous signs so any inherited danger can be named and disciplined early rather than discovered by disaster. Edrin begins doing so through observation of Raven’s pressure response and the way his arcane techniques answer best through instinct aligned with calm rather than through force.

Scene 12 — Find Familiar becomes the whole argument
Find Familiar becomes the session’s core spell. Raven wants it because it represents companionship, responsibility, structure, and a safer kind of boundary-crossing than the tears. Edrin keeps circling around burden, compatibility, respect, and safe invitation. Raven and his mother both become increasingly impatient with lesson-shaped circles that threaten not to become real instruction. Raven compares Edrin’s pacing to being held just outside striking range by a better fighter.

Scene 13 — The threshold fight and the end of fog
Raven finally forces Edrin to answer why “one more lesson” is needed. He points out that Edrin already knows Raven is ready enough to begin, that spellbooks cost more coin than a poor boy can quickly earn, and that delaying forever is fear dressed as care. Edrin admits Raven is right: the true thing missing was not Raven’s readiness but Edrin’s willingness to become the man who actually taught the spell. This changes the teaching. The threshold is crossed.

Scene 14 — Opening shape of Find Familiar
Once Edrin stops hiding inside the promise of future teaching, Raven learns the first real opening shape of Find Familiar. He fixes the purpose as not ownership, not vanity, and not a tool dressed as a friend, but an honest companion bond that is enduring and mutual. He then fixes three boundaries: no answer rooted in coercion, domination, or false obedience; no answer carrying taint that corrupts purpose; and no answer sustained by hunger, flattery, or projection. Finally, he fixes the first conditional call: “Let only that which can answer within these bounds answer.” Edrin confirms that this is a true opening shape, not just theory, and sets the next lesson as formal structure and first casting preparation.

Closing State
Raven ends Session 001 still without a learned wizard spell, but no longer being asked to mistake indefinite preparation for teaching. He has work, scraps of paper, a growing plan for building a spellbook, clearer knowledge of his parents’ past, a much sharper sense of Edrin’s wound and value as a teacher, and the first lawful opening form of Find Familiar fixed cleanly in memory. The next lesson is formally defined: not another circle, but structure and first casting preparation.
