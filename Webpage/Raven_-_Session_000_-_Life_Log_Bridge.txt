Session 000 Opening Position

Raven's recorded life in Session 000 begins at age 4 with the lived scene of his father's death. This is the first played event in his archive, not only a memory later described from a distance. It stands at the front of his lived chronology even though the rest of Session 000 later follows Raven in later childhood.

The session therefore has a split historical shape: an opening wound at age 4, then a later-present line around age 12 where Raven trains, explores, questions, and begins to define how he will meet danger.

First Loss - Age 4

Raven's father dies in a scene the player actually played through. The death is tied to magic and is understood through the image of a man burned from within or destroyed by internal magical backlash. Raven is too young to understand the full mechanics of what happened, but the experience brands the event into his life as lived truth rather than secondhand family history.

That moment does not merely explain Raven later. It is itself part of Raven's life on the page. The archive should treat it as an anchor event: the first rupture, the beginning of his fear of uncontrolled power, and the earliest root of his need to understand how dangerous magic ends people.

Mother, Household, and Survival

Raven grows in a practical, survival-first household shaped by scarcity, work, and discipline. His mother trains him not through soft instruction but through expectation, pressure, and tests that often blur the line between lesson and ordeal. Protection in this house is real, but it is delivered in the form of competence.

Her approach teaches Raven to watch, adapt, and stay alive. It also teaches him that survival is not elegant. Food, repairs, work, and the cost of daily life matter. This grounding keeps Raven's curiosity from floating free of the material world.

Later Childhood Baseline

By the later-present portion of Session 000, Raven is still a child, but no longer an unshaped one. He has combat habits, caution, and enough stubbornness to push beyond the first lesson handed to him. He does not yet stand on finalized class language. What matters more at this stage is that he refuses to choose between blade and magic as though one must erase the other.

He learns through observation, trial, and the limits imposed by adults who want to keep him alive. That combination makes him curious without being reckless in a simple way.

Early Relationship to Magic

Raven's relationship to magic is not formalized in Session 000, but it is already intense. Magic is both attraction and threat. He is drawn to it because it may explain loss, danger, and the strange wounds in reality that the session introduces. He fears it because his father's death ties magical power to catastrophe before Raven has any reason to trust it.

He does not frame magic as easy power. He wants understanding, control, and the ability to end or survive what should not be loose. Even here, before later canon refines the idea, his interest leans toward disciplined knowledge rather than surrender.

Forest, Tears, and Thin Places

Session 000 establishes the forest as both ordinary living space and dangerous edge. Raven explores it with increasing independence, and the forest becomes the place where practical life and metaphysical danger overlap. It is not just background scenery. It is the boundary line where the known world begins to fray.

In that space Raven encounters a tear or thin place in reality. The session teaches him one of the central laws that will matter later: observation invites interaction. The unseen is not passively watched. Attention can become contact.

Control, Refusal, and First Teaching

Raven's growth in Session 000 does not come through overpowering the strange. It comes through boundary, control, and refusal. During the Thin Place incident he stabilizes himself and helps another child hold herself together rather than winning by direct force. This is a defining shape for him: survival through internal steadiness rather than domination.

The session also includes an early teaching moment. Raven does not simply endure knowledge; he begins to pass on what he has learned. That matters because it marks a shift from private reaction to outward responsibility.

People Established in Session 000

Raven's mother is the clearest fixed person in his early life: trainer, authority figure, protector, and pressure source all at once. His father, though dead, remains active in the archive through the force of his loss and the unanswered nature of that death.

Edrin enters as a cautious mentor figure connected to arcane study, tears, and careful boundaries. Other children matter less as permanent biographies and more as mirrors that reveal Raven's limits, instincts, and willingness to intervene.

What Session 000 Leaves Open

Session 000 does not settle formal class identity, subclass, exact mechanics of magic, or any later polished version of Raven's build. It does not answer what exactly killed his father, what tears fully are, or how safely magic can be mastered.

It leaves Raven with stronger practical control, deeper awareness of thin places, and larger questions instead of clean closure. Those uncertainties must remain uncertainties in this file.

Anchor Events for Later Continuity

The father's death at age 4 is an anchor event. The discovery of tears and thin places is an anchor event. The lesson that observation can become interaction is an anchor event. The Thin Place stabilization and first teaching moment are anchor events.

Later sessions may deepen these events through continuity echoes, but this bridge file preserves their Session 000 shape without importing later interpretation as if it had already happened.
