2. Start State

Raven begins the session as a 12-year-old fighter-wizard in the Neverwinter forest orbit, already committed to both blade and arcane growth, already carrying the anchor wound of his father's magical death, and already operating under a practical household economy. Edrin is the current arcane mentor but is still teaching mostly through caution, boundaries, and tear-focused field work rather than clean formal spell instruction. Find Familiar is already the desired first real wizard spell, but is not yet learned.

3. End State

Age 12; Level 4; Fighter 2 / Wizard 2; XP 5625 / 6500; Current Coin 18 cp and 12 sp; no learned wizard spells yet; Find Familiar in active study.

Narrative end state: Raven has forced Edrin to move from vague caution into explicit Find Familiar groundwork. The opening purpose, three boundaries, and first conditional call are fixed cleanly. The next lesson is explicitly formal structure and first casting preparation for Find Familiar.

4. Scene-by-Scene Event Chain

4.1 Forest opening and map challenge

Raven meets Edrin, challenges the rough tear map, questions scale, and refuses to accept warning without usable navigation language. This establishes Raven's teaching style conflict immediately.

4.2 First tear field reading

At the split-boulder site Raven uses Soft Awareness to read the tear. He identifies wound-like qualities, an attentional pull, suppression of surrounding reality, and an inward-healing or collapsing quality rather than active widening.

4.3 Early magical theory conflict

Raven challenges Edrin on why the magic he teaches does not resemble the forms Raven has seen in the library. Edrin admits Raven's magic does not fit tidy formal structures cleanly and may answer through different channels.

4.4 Return walk and questioning about the Weave

Raven presses on whether his magic resists him, resists the Weave, or is simply shaped differently. Edrin answers with layered theory rather than direct formulas, deepening Raven's frustration.

4.5 Homecoming and mother dialogue

Raven returns home and speaks with his mother about Edrin, magic, trust, and the shape of danger. She confirms that she distrusts both magic and men who teach it to boys, but does not ban Edrin outright.

4.6 Father-loss deepening

Raven reveals that he remembers his father's death as magical burning from within and repeats his father's last words, "not just blade." Mother later confirms she and Raven's father were adventurers, that he likely had sorcerous talent, and that his death likely came from magical failure or backlash.

4.7 Mother-Edrin comparison and training judgment

Raven and his mother work through the difference between caution, cowardice, pacing, and real teaching. She encourages him to ask smaller, sharper tool-questions and to demand clarity rather than slogans.

4.8 Work and resource pressure

Raven seeks work, finds steady stable labor and occasional library tasks, earns coin, and begins thinking practically about buying or constructing a spellbook. He also acquires scrap paper for notes.

4.9 Sorcery suspicion and Edrin's scar

Raven concludes at least one tear is Edrin's fault and confronts him about whether he still trusts his own magic. Edrin admits the split-boulder tear is tied to his own failed boundary-contact working and that he has been teaching from the scar of that failure.

4.10 Find Familiar groundwork, burden, and boundaries

Edrin teaches burden categories, safe invitation logic, compatibility vs projection, and the need for a bond that is mutual rather than coercive. Raven repeatedly drags the teaching back to Find Familiar when Edrin drifts.

4.11 Threshold confrontation

Raven finally forces Edrin to answer why "one more lesson" was required. Edrin admits that the real barrier was his own reluctance to become the man who actually taught the spell, not any missing readiness in Raven.

4.12 Opening shape fixed

Raven learns the opening purpose, boundaries, and conditional call of Find Familiar, repeats them cleanly, and secures an explicit promise that the next lesson is formal structure and first casting preparation.

5. Major Revelations and Continuity Weight

Edrin confirms that at least one tear near the split boulder is tied to his own failed boundary-contact spellwork.

Raven's mother confirms that she and Raven's father were adventurers and that his arcane talent was likely sorcerous rather than wizardly.

Raven's father's last remembered words to him are "not just blade."

Mother explicitly asks Edrin to test Raven for possible inherited sorcerous traits.

Raven and Edrin fix the first clean opening shape of Find Familiar rather than only speaking around it.

6. Emotional / Psychological Developments

Raven becomes more willing to speak directly about his father's death, the magical nature of the wound, and the fear that inherited sorcery could someday turn him into the same kind of self-destruction. He also becomes more willing to challenge teachers directly rather than mistaking politeness for patience. The session clarifies that Raven's impatience is not simple childish wanting; it is partly the frustration of someone who already lives with real danger being treated as though he only imagines it.

7. Mechanical / Material Changes

Exact end-state values should route through the Character Sheet, but at the confirmed end boundary Raven stands at Level 4, Fighter 2 / Wizard 2, with 5625 XP and 18 cp / 12 sp. He still has no learned wizard spells yet. Find Familiar is under active study, with the next lesson set to formal structure and first casting preparation. He also acquires paper scraps for notes and continues working toward a spellbook.
