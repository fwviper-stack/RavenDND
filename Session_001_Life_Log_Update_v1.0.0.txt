Session Opening Position

Session 001 opens with Raven at age 12 under active Raven Protocol governance, carrying the formative history of Session 000 into his first fully protocol-era arc. He begins this session already split between blade and magic, already shaped by the death of his father, and already living inside a household where his mother measures safety in competence rather than comfort. He enters the session with active tension around tears in the forest, Edrin's caution, and the desire to study real spellcraft without being treated as a child too fragile to hold danger honestly.

First Day of Session 1 — Forest Lesson and Tear Reading

Raven begins Session 1 by meeting Edrin at the shelter and pressing immediately against vague instruction. He challenges Edrin's rough map, questions scale, and refuses to accept warning without useful measurements. That sets the tone for the whole session: Raven is not only curious, but resistant to lazy teaching. Together they go to the nearest marked tear near a split boulder and white-barked trees, where Raven uses Soft Awareness to read the anomaly. He identifies the tear as wound-like rather than simply open, senses that it draws sound, calm, and attention inward, recognizes that it is anchored yet not perfectly still, and concludes that it feels more like a wound slowly healing or pulling inward than one actively widening. He also recognizes that its most dangerous quality is the subtle pull on attention itself.

Edrin and the First Serious Arcane Friction

The forest lesson becomes the first major strain-point between Raven and Edrin. Raven keeps asking not only what a tear does, but why Edrin teaches him in fragments and abstractions rather than in the clear structures he sees in formal books. He admits that the library's idea of the Weave feels different from the kinds of techniques Edrin has been teaching him. He also identifies, correctly, that hearing too much from Edrin before making his own first reading would contaminate his perception. Over the course of the walk and the return, Raven pushes Edrin hard enough that the old wizard finally admits several truths: Raven's magic does not fit formal structures cleanly yet; at least one tear near the split boulder is tied to Edrin's own failed boundary-contact work; and some of Edrin's caution belongs to his own scar before it belongs to Raven.

Father, Mother, and the Shape of Magic

One of the deepest developments of the session happens when Raven speaks openly—first with Edrin and then with his mother—about his father. Raven confirms that he remembers his father's death as a magical wound, "burned from within," and repeats his father's last words to him: "not just blade." His mother later confirms that she and Raven's father really did live an adventuring life together, that Raven's father had arcane talent closer to sorcery than wizardry, and that he was gifted until he was not. The session therefore deepens the father-loss anchor event from Session 000: the father's death is not only magical catastrophe in the abstract, but increasingly framed as a likely self-carried or backlash-like arcane failure. This does not make Raven abandon magic; instead it clarifies why control, shutdown, and survival matter so much to him.

Raven and His Mother — Trust, Fear, and Hard Approval

The return home creates one of the session's strongest emotional lines. Raven and his mother speak more openly than they have before about his father, her old life, and the risk of arcane study. She confirms that she and Raven's father were adventurers, that she stayed while he kept going longer than he should have, and that she distrusts not only magic but any teacher who holds influence over her son. Even so, she does not forbid Edrin. Instead she evaluates him with the same severe practicality she applies to everything else. She also becomes the person who helps Raven sharpen his judgment of Edrin: she pushes him to distinguish patience from stagnation, safety from vagueness, and a real threshold from endless preparation. By session's end she implicitly trusts Edrin enough to ask him to test Raven for possible inherited sorcerous signs—but not enough to stop watching him closely.

Work, Coin, and Practical Life

Session 001 is not only arcane conversation. Raven works. He finds steady small labor at the stables and occasional useful tasks at the library. The labor matters for more than coin. Stable work grounds him physically, gives him honest exhaustion, and makes abstract frustration simpler to carry. Library work matters because it puts him close to books, paper scraps, and the kind of life he may one day choose for himself. Through this practical pressure, Raven begins thinking seriously about the true cost of a spellbook and the possibility of building one by hand if he cannot afford a finished volume. That thought matters because it shows Raven beginning to think like a maker, not only a buyer or dreamer.

Find Familiar Becomes the Session's Central Spell-Question

Across the second half of the session, Find Familiar becomes the spell around which Raven and Edrin's whole teaching conflict crystallizes. Raven wants not only the spell, but the structure and dignity of being taught it honestly. Edrin delays, circles, and grounds the teaching in questions of burden, compatibility, and safe invitation. Raven and his mother both grow frustrated with lessons that threaten to become all scaffolding and no wall. Eventually Raven forces Edrin to stop hiding inside caution and admit that he has been teaching from the scar of his own failure. That confrontation changes the teaching. Raven no longer receives only slogans about caution; he begins receiving the real opening structure of the spell.

The Opening Shape of Find Familiar

By the session's close, Raven successfully learns and recites the opening shape of the Find Familiar invitation. He fixes the purpose as not ownership, not vanity, and not a tool dressed as a friend, but an honest companion bond that is enduring and mutual. He then fixes three boundaries: no answer rooted in coercion, domination, or false obedience; no answer carrying taint that corrupts purpose; and no answer sustained by hunger, flattery, or projection. Finally, he fixes the opening call itself: "Let only that which can answer within these bounds answer." This is not the full spell, but it is no longer only theory. It is the first lawful opening form.

Session Closing Position

Session 001 closes with Raven in a stronger but more exact position than he began. He has not yet cast Find Familiar, but he has forced the road to it into honesty. He has confirmed that his mother and Edrin both think possible inherited sorcery should be tested, that Edrin's caution is partly personal scar, and that the next lesson is no longer vague groundwork but formal structure and first casting preparation for Find Familiar. He ends the session still poor, still working, still child-young, but no longer being managed as though waiting without end is the same thing as teaching.
