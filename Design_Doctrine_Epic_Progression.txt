Epic Progression Design Log
Before the story began, before Raven stepped into the forest or faced the tear, there was a
discussion about limits.
Specifically, the level cap.
The idea of stopping at level 20 did not sit right. Growth should not simply end because a system
says it should. A character like Raven, who adapts and evolves, would not reach a point where
progression simply halts.
So the question became:
If there is no level cap, what happens when a character becomes too powerful for the world they
exist in?
The answer was not to artificially limit the character.
The answer was to let the world fall behind.
Power, in this system, is not constrained by numbers alone. It changes the relationship between the
character and reality itself. At a certain point, Raven would not simply become stronger—he would
become incompatible with the limitations of his current realm.
This led to the core concept:
Outgrowing the realm.
As Raven gains levels beyond what the world was designed to contain, several things begin to
happen.
The environment reacts differently. Creatures that once posed a threat become insignificant. Magic
behaves differently around him, not because he is casting stronger spells, but because his
presence alone begins to influence the rules.
Eventually, the world is no longer the right place for him.
Not because he is forced out.
But because he no longer fits within it.
At that point, progression does not stop. Instead, the scale changes.
New realms. New rules. New threats that exist at a level appropriate to what Raven has become.
This avoids the problem of infinite scaling within a static environment. Instead of inflating numbers
endlessly, the context evolves.
The system becomes:
Grow → Outgrow → Transition → Continue.

This design keeps progression meaningful while preserving tension. Raven does not become
untouchable—he becomes something that must face different kinds of challenges.
This concept became the foundation for Rule 2.
No level cap.
Because the story does not end when the numbers stop.
It changes when the world can no longer contain you.
